Climate Change

The defining environmental challenge of our time

The Science of Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These shifts may be natural, but since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas, which produces heat-trapping gases.

1.1°C
Global temperature increase since pre-industrial times
410 ppm
Current atmospheric CO₂ concentration
3.3 mm
Annual sea level rise

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), which includes more than 1,300 scientists from the United States and other countries, forecasts a temperature rise of 2.5 to 10 degrees Fahrenheit over the next century.

According to the IPCC, the extent of climate change effects on individual regions will vary over time and with the ability of different societal and environmental systems to mitigate or adapt to change.

Key Solutions

  • Transition to renewable energy sources like solar and wind
  • Improve energy efficiency in buildings, transportation, and industry
  • Protect and restore carbon sinks like forests and wetlands
  • Adopt sustainable agricultural practices
  • Implement carbon pricing mechanisms

Plastic Pollution

A growing threat to our oceans and ecosystems

The Plastic Crisis

Plastic pollution has become one of the most pressing environmental issues, as rapidly increasing production of disposable plastic products overwhelms the world's ability to deal with them. Plastic pollution is most visible in developing Asian and African nations, where garbage collection systems are often inefficient or nonexistent.

8M
Tons of plastic entering oceans annually
400
Years for plastic to degrade
100K+
Marine animals killed by plastic yearly

Microplastics—tiny pieces of broken-down plastic smaller than your fingernail—have been found in tap water, beer, salt, and are present in all oceans around the world. Researchers have also found microplastics in human blood, with unknown health effects.

Single-use plastics account for 40 percent of the plastic produced every year. Many of these products, such as plastic bags and food wrappers, have a lifespan of mere minutes to hours, yet they may persist in the environment for hundreds of years.

Key Solutions

  • Reduce consumption of single-use plastics
  • Improve waste management and recycling infrastructure
  • Develop and use biodegradable alternatives
  • Implement extended producer responsibility policies
  • Support cleanup efforts in rivers and oceans

Deforestation

The destruction of Earth's forests and its consequences

Forest Loss and Its Impact

Deforestation is the purposeful clearing of forested land. Throughout history and into modern times, forests have been razed to make space for agriculture and animal grazing, and to obtain wood for fuel, manufacturing, and construction.

4.7M
Hectares of forest lost annually
80%
Of Earth's land animals and plants live in forests
15%
Of greenhouse gas emissions come from deforestation

Forests cover about 30 percent of the planet's land mass, but humans are cutting them down, clearing these essential habitats on a massive scale. Since 1990, half of the world's rainforests have been destroyed. At the current rate of deforestation, the world's rainforests could completely vanish within 100 years.

The biggest driver of deforestation is agriculture. Farmers cut forests to provide more room for planting crops or grazing livestock. Often, small farmers will clear a few acres by cutting down trees and burning them in a process known as slash and burn agriculture.

Key Solutions

  • Implement sustainable forestry practices
  • Establish and enforce protected areas
  • Support reforestation and afforestation efforts
  • Promote sustainable agriculture to reduce expansion
  • Create economic incentives for forest conservation

Environmental Timeline

Key events and milestones in the global environmental movement

1962

Silent Spring

Rachel Carson publishes "Silent Spring," documenting the harmful effects of pesticides on the environment, particularly birds. The book is widely credited with launching the modern environmental movement.

1970

First Earth Day

The first Earth Day is celebrated on April 22, mobilizing 20 million Americans to demonstrate for environmental protection. This event led to the creation of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency.

1987

Montreal Protocol

The Montreal Protocol is signed, aiming to phase out substances that deplete the ozone layer. It is considered one of the most successful international environmental agreements to date.

1997

Kyoto Protocol

The Kyoto Protocol is adopted, committing industrialized countries to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. It was the first agreement of its kind to set legally binding emissions reduction targets.

2015

Paris Agreement

The Paris Agreement is adopted, bringing nations together to combat climate change and adapt to its effects. It aims to limit global warming to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels.

2018

School Strike for Climate

Greta Thunberg initiates the "School Strike for Climate" movement, inspiring millions of young people worldwide to demand stronger action on climate change from political leaders.

Take Action

Everyone has a role to play in addressing environmental challenges. Here are some ways you can make a difference:

Individual Actions

Reduce your carbon footprint by using public transportation, eating less meat, minimizing single-use plastics, and conserving energy and water at home.

Support sustainable businesses and products that prioritize environmental responsibility. Your purchasing decisions send powerful signals to the market.

Community Engagement

Participate in local environmental initiatives such as community clean-ups, tree planting events, and educational workshops.

Advocate for sustainable policies in your community by attending town halls, contacting elected officials, and supporting environmental organizations.

Global Advocacy

Stay informed about environmental issues and share accurate information with your networks to raise awareness.

Support international agreements and organizations working to address global environmental challenges through donations, volunteering, or amplifying their messages.
